The Problem

Your team has the craft. The way you source talent, tag and track notes, log time-coded recommendations, manage follow-up, build call sheets, and revisit a name when the right project lands — that process is what makes the work great. It lives in spreadsheets, inboxes, and the senior people's heads. That works at one studio's scale. It breaks the moment you try to expand reach, ramp a new coordinator, or run more projects in parallel. The fix isn't more headcount or another off-the-shelf tool — it's encoding the process you already trust.

The Approach
01

Your process is the spec

We don't impose a template or a generic ATS. We document the way your team actually works — and that becomes what we build.

02

Scale reach without losing taste

The point isn't replacing curation. It's making sure no good name slips through, follow-ups happen on time, and prior decisions stay searchable across every project.

03

Training as a byproduct

When the system holds the process, new hires ramp by working in it — not shadowing for two years. The platform becomes the apprenticeship.

04

Operating leverage, not workflow software

Built for how casting actually moves: tagging at time-code, call sheets generated from decisions, recommendations that resurface when the right project lands. Not a CRM bent into shape.

What You Walk Away With
  • A sourcing pipeline that compounds with every project, not resets

  • Scheduling and call sheets generated from your decisions, not rebuilt each project
  • Follow-up and re-evaluation surfaced at the right moment, not lost in inboxes
  • New hires productive in weeks because the system holds the craft
  • A platform for expansion — new offices, new project types, new partnerships
