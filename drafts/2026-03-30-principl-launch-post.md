## Voice Note

Both hooks lead with recognition, not criticism. The reader should feel seen, not called out. Hook A is more direct and pattern-interrupt ("most businesses have the same problem"). Hook B is more introspective and aspirational ("there's a version of your business"). Both avoid leading with "I" per LinkedIn rules and Danny's voice guide.

The anti-course framing ("not a course, not a mastermind, not someone handing you a playbook") appears in both but as a single beat, not the headline. It handles the objection without making the post about what the program isn't.

No proof points in either hook. This is intentional. The launch post earns the click through resonance with the pain, not credentials. The principl.ai landing page carries the credibility load (the $3M solar build, the $30M lesson, the university teaching). Stacking proof points in a LinkedIn post when people haven't asked "why should I trust you" yet just reads as insecure.

Price is included deliberately. $8,000 is a filter. Anyone who clicks through after seeing the number is a real lead. Anyone who bounces was never going to apply.

The first comment is designed for easy sharing. "Send this their way" gives the network permission to tag or forward without feeling like they're doing a sales pitch.

Photo note: attach a photo that isn't a headshot or a branded graphic. Something real. Office, whiteboard, coffee shop where you actually work. The editorial design of the site should do the branding work, not the LinkedIn thumbnail.
