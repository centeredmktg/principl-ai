A proposal for Colorado Plastic Surgery

Your footage is an asset that's stuck as a backlog.

A system that turns hours of unbroken surgical and consult footage into a cut-ready Premiere timeline, plus a searchable record of every case, before your editor has even finished loading the files.

What I already see

I looked at how you actually shoot.

Before I pitch anything, I want to lay out my read on how you shoot, so you can see this is built around your actual room rather than a generic tool hunting for a problem.

The OR
Four cameras in the room plus a handheld iPhone, and the doctor wears two lav mics, one running into the computer and the other into the phone.
Consult rooms
Two to three cameras, with the doctor's lav, the coordinator's lav, and a room mic each sitting on its own channel.
The sessions
Long, unbroken streams that cover an entire procedure, sometimes running up to twenty hours of HD multicam.
The real bottleneck
The slow part isn't your editor's skill, it's the time spent scrubbing footage to find the moments, and the render time on terabytes of HD before anyone can even begin.
The shift

The audio already knows where the moments are.

The doctor narrates the whole procedure, and that narration is really a map of every cut-worthy moment in the footage, one we simply haven't been reading yet.

The thing that makes this work is that audio is a tiny fraction of the data. A twenty-hour HD shoot runs to terabytes of video, but its audio is only a few hundred megabytes, so we can pull that audio out in minutes without rendering, transcoding, or even opening Premiere, then put the smart work to it while the heavy footage loads in the background.

Why this matters

The thinking runs ahead of the rendering.

By the time Premiere finishes chewing through the footage, the list of marked moments is already sitting there waiting, so your editor's first touch on the timeline is cut-aware from the start, with far less scrubbing and far less back-and-forth.

The system

How a moment becomes a marker.

Pull and transcribe the audio

We extract each audio channel and transcribe it with word-level timing, and because each mic sits on its own channel, we already know who's speaking by physics, since the doctor's lav is the doctor. That becomes your master transcript.

Runs locally · patient audio never leaves your environment

Flag the moments worth cutting

The system reads through the transcript and surfaces candidate moments, tagging each one for its audience, whether that's consumer (the hook, the story, the transformation) or educational (the technique, the teaching aside), and giving each a one-line reason.

Recommends · never auto-cuts

You confirm what tells the story

You review the flagged moments against the transcript, play the audio inline, and keep what's gold while dropping what isn't. The judgment stays yours, and the system simply handles the scrubbing for you.

You decide the story

Markers land in Premiere

Confirmed moments export straight onto the timeline as markers that are labeled, timed, and synced across every camera angle, including the handheld iPhone, so the editor opens a project that's already mapped.

Validated against Adobe's Premiere API
Two details that make it yours

Built for how a surgeon actually works.

◎

A spoken shortcut: "mark that"

Say a simple phrase during the procedure, something like "mark that," and the system automatically flags that moment as highest priority. You get to pre-approve the moments you know are gold right as they happen, without breaking your rhythm, while the system goes looking for the ones you didn't call out.

▤

Every case becomes searchable

The same transcript that speeds up today's edit gets saved as a structured, searchable record, so three years from now you can pull every case where you discussed a given technique, whether that's for a book, a lecture, a teaching library, or a quick reference before a similar procedure. The edit gets faster today, and the archive keeps compounding in value as the library grows.

A note on patient privacy

This is patient footage, so the system is designed privacy-first, with transcription running on your own machine by default and nothing shipped off to a third-party service. You've clearly handled releases for your existing content already, so think of this as me showing you that the design respects that same line from the very start.

How we'd build it

Start where the footage is clean, then scale it into the room where the time savings are biggest.

Phase 1 · Prove the system

The consult rooms

  • Cleaner, shorter footage with each speaker already on a separate channel
  • Stand up the full pipeline end-to-end: transcript → flagged moments → your review → Premiere markers
  • Build your moment criteria into the system so it learns what you consider cut-worthy
  • Every session saved to your searchable case archive
Phase 2 · Scale to the hard case

The operating room

  • Sync the handheld iPhone to the four-camera rig automatically, using the doctor's voice as the anchor
  • Handle the 20-hour streams where the time savings are biggest
  • A second pass that reads across all the marked moments for the bigger story arc
  • "I'm cutting a 90-second rhinoplasty explainer" → moments flagged against that brief
