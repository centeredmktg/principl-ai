§ 01 / The Problem

The constraint isn't strategy. It's that nobody wants to run a tech company.

The casting and talent business runs on decades of relationships and pattern recognition that can't be shortcut. The transition into development and production needs operational scaffolding the team didn't sign up to build.

Project pipelines, talent intelligence, packaging workflows, financier and distribution tracking — most of it lives in spreadsheets, group texts, and the heads of the principals. It works until it doesn't.

Studios that try to build software end up dragging the principals into running a tech company. Wrong direction.

§ 02 / The Approach

Four moves. The principal stays in taste.

The methodology is opinionated about what software is for, and what it isn't. Anchor to the irreplaceable asset, build connective tissue (not consumer products), keep the principal in their craft, compound the relationship graph.

01

Anchor to the irreplaceable asset.

Your taste, your relationships, your pattern matching for who breaks out. Software exists to scale that, not replace it. We design around the principal's judgment — not around it.

02

Connective tissue, not consumer products.

Workflow infrastructure for a small team that needs to operate like a much bigger one. Talent intelligence, packaging, relationship CRM. Internal tools that compound. Not apps for the open market.

03

The principal stays in taste.

We run the build and the platform so the people with the eye stay focused on the work only they can do. No principal pulled into running a tech company.

04

Everything compounds the relationship graph.

Every workflow feeds the same record of talent, projects, financiers, and buyers. The longer you run it, the more durable the asset. CRM-of-record for a studio — not generic SaaS.
